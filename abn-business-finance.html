FAQ

Common business finance questions

How long does my ABN need to be active?
For most low-doc options, from 6 months. Full-doc deals (with financials) need even less. For new ABNs under 6 months, we have specialist lenders that consider industry trading history.
Do I need to provide financials?
Not always. Low-doc and bank-statement lenders approve based on business activity, not tax returns. We assess your situation first and only request what's actually needed.
What's the maximum I can borrow?
Asset-dependent. Single-asset low-doc up to $250K is common. Larger amounts ($250K–$1M+) usually need financials or asset-backing. We've settled multi-asset deals into 7 figures.
What's a balloon and should I use one?
A balloon (or residual) is a lump sum at the end of your loan term that lowers regular repayments. Common on vehicles and yellow goods. It improves cashflow but you'll pay more interest, or need to refinance/sell at term end. We model both scenarios for you.
Will applying affect my credit?
We use soft pre-assessments before any formal credit enquiry. Multiple direct lender applications hurt your file — funnelling through us protects it.
Can I finance private sales or auction purchases?
Yes. We regularly settle private sales, auction purchases and interstate deals. We handle PPSR checks, encumbrance clearance and direct payment to the seller.
Is there a fee for your service?
Our pre-approval process is cost-free and commitment-free — there's nothing to pay to see your options. If you choose to proceed, an origination fee applies; it's only ever payable from the proceeds of your loan contract, so nothing comes out of your pocket. Because every lender's pricing is slightly different, we'll walk you through all fees upfront once your pre-approval is in place — no surprises.
